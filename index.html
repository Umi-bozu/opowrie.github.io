Optimised Lightweight Glider

Designed and built a glider with a 3D-printed, weight-optimised aerofoil using a bird-bone internal lattice structure — balancing aerodynamic performance with structural efficiency across a 1m wingspan.

Launched from approximately 2m altitude, the glider flew 7m — demonstrating effective lift-to-drag optimisation.

  • 1m wingspan
  • 7m flight distance from ~2m launch altitude
  • Bird-bone internal lattice aerofoil structure

CubeSat Payload Frame

Designed and validated a lightweight CubeSat payload structure for integration into a rocket payload bay — optimised for minimum mass while meeting tight dimensional constraints.

Validated via CAD, machining, and ANSYS FEA. Passed fit and load acceptance testing on first attempt. Also identified a critical integration issue during documentation and coordinated the corrective redesign that enabled the payload to fly.

  • Mass-minimised design within dimensional constraints
  • First-pass fit and load acceptance testing
  • Critical integration issue caught and resolved pre-flight
